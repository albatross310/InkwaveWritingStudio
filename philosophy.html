Philosophy

Writing tools optimise for frictionless output. Inkwave asks whether the friction is the point.

Writing that remembers

Every word you choose leaves a trace. Inkwave makes that trace visible — not as surveillance, but as a mnemonic record of how your thinking moved.

Constraints as craft

Each session draws a random exclusion set. When a word falls outside it, Inkwave makes a suggestion. You swap, keep, or delete — and that small act of deliberation becomes part of the record.

Mnemonic pictures

As you write, your choices are distilled into beautiful picture tiles — a visual, mnemonic summary of how your document took shape. Each tile is unique to your session: a quiet portrait of the decisions behind the words.

Private by construction

Your writing stays in your browser. Nothing is stored on our servers. We cannot read your work because we never receive it.

Solo now, together soon

Inkwave Solo is live and free. Inkwave Cubed, the collaborative environment built on the same mnemonic philosophy and picture tiles, is in development.
